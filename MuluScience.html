My neuroscience research has focused on :

  • Robotic touch sensation (artificial perception)
  • Somatosensory Neuroprosthesis (Brain-machine interfacing)
  • Spatial (visual) memory & perception

Main methods

  • Single/Multi unit recording

  • Microstimulation

  • Tactile stimulation

  • Structural MRI

  • EMG

  • Careful behavioral measurements such as eye positions, arm movement kinematics/dynamics

The main results of my investigations include :

  1. Compared to the cortex, the ventral posterolateral (VPL) thalamus could be an equal or better candidate for creating natural sensations via somatosensory neuroprosthesis. i.e. to send feedback from brain machine interfacing (BMI) devices to the brain by using electrical stimulation to replicate natural brain activity found that:

  2. There are stimulus-dependent dynamic interactions between the cortex and the VPL thalamus (as measured by Linear Granger causality (LGC) analysis to investigate information flow)

  3. The lateral intraparietal area (LIP) has access to a supraretinal representation similar to that found in the medial temporal lobe, from which it can extract the vector representation of environmental memory. This signal maybe important (missing) in patients with parietal lesions and some Schizophrenics, who have difficulty recalling familiar objects from memory.
