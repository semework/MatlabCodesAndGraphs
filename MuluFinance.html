This is a program that calculates your financial gain based on a few parameters. In short, you invest a given amount of money in the stock market and you wait for the price to go up or down.

In our program, you can slide the circle, or type your amount, or use the up and down arrows in the first field (above: Investment (US$) =).

Then, depending on the number of day, days, weeks, months, etc. that the investment is priced again, you will get a return (more money) or a loss (your investment is not giving a good result). There is a limited amount of money that your investment grows by, which is called "interest rate". For instance, if you invested 100 dollars and in a week you gain 3 more dollars, your interest rate is 3 percent (%).

You can change this rate by using the slider/arrows/ or typing in the area above:Interest (%).

Obviously, you have to pay a certain amount of money to the government from this income, in what is called "tax".

The bottom slider adjusts how often your money grows per a given time (in this case a year.) This is called compound frequency. More numbers mean more money for you and the tax collector.
